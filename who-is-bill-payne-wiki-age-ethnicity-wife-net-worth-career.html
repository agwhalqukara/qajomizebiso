Bill Payne Biography

Billy Payne was born William Porter Payne on October 13, 1947, in Athens, Georgia, United States. He played for the University of Georgia as a sophomore in 1996. He earned a Bachelor of Arts in political science with honors in 1969. He then earned his law degree from the School of Law in 1973. Then, in 1991, he received an honorary Doctor of Laws degree from Oglethorpe University.

Career

Payne dreamed of hosting the Olympics in Atlanta in 1987, and he sought support from all over the world. Then he got the backing of then-mayor Andrew Young, as well as private support and sponsors. The IOC chose Atlanta to host the 1996 Olympics, much to his surprise. Even after winning the bid, he remained the chairman of the Atlanta Olympic Committee.

Payne was named chairman of Augusta National Golf Club, home of the Master’s Tournament, on May 5, 2006, succeeding Hootie Johnson. Payne began working for the club at the start of the season in October of the same year.

Payne criticized Tiger Woods before that year’s Masters on April 7, 2010, claiming that he had failed as a role model.

The following year, Payne and his fellow Augusta National members implemented another modern change to their featured golf tournament. They also authorized the video game “Tiger Woods PGA Tour 12,” which was designed to inspire future generations of golfers. The proceeds from the video game’s sales would go to a non-profit foundation that promotes young golfers.

On August 23, 2017, Augusta National announced that Payne would step down as chairman of the club effective October 16, 2017, and would be succeeded by Fred Ridley.

He began his career as the chairman of Centennial Holding Company, based in Atlanta. The company was dealing with fund management issues.

Bill Payne Wife, Marriage

Bill Payne received the Olympic Order in Gold in 1996 at the Atlanta Games’ Closing Ceremonies. In addition, he was elected as a Georgia Trustee in 2014. He was inducted into the World Golf Hall of Fame in the lifetime achievement category in the class of 2019.

Aside from that, one of his greatest achievements was persuading members of the International Olympic Committee to hold the Olympics in Atlanta, a dream that was realized in 1996. He also pushed for the first women to be admitted to the club’s membership, which had never been done before.
