Career

David Yost, after completing his education, moved to California to pursue his acting career. Within just three months after arriving, he got the role of Billy Cranston in the television series Mighty Morphin Power Rangers. In the first four seasons of the show, he starred in 200 episodes and was the only cast member to appear in every single episode of the original series.

In 1995, Yost appeared in the famous movie Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: The Movie, which made $60 million at the box office. In the fourth season of Power Rangers Zeo, his character became a technical advisor to the others. At the end of the season, he left the show, and a tribute to his character was seen in the closing credits of the final episode. Yost left the series because he was harassed by the production crew, who targeted him over his sexual orientation.

Yost appeared in the 1995 movie, Scene of the Crime as Josh White. In the 2000 television film, After Different Strokes: When the Laughter Stopped, he played the role of a Playboy photographer. In the same year, he was an associate producer for the television film, The Mary Kay Letourneau Story. In 2001, he produced two TV series, Alien Hunter and Temptation Island. Yost was the director of the production of the science fiction show, Sci-Fi Lab.

Yost became the manager of licensing of the production company, Geneon, which sold television shows and films to such networks as Showtime, Starz, Encore, and Cartoon Network. In 2002, he was seen in the play Fallen Guardian Angels in Los Angeles that raised $25,000 for The Children’s Hospitals AIDS Center. In 2004, Yost produced the English documentary series You Are What You Eat. He appeared in the NOH8 Campaign, the Anime Festival Orlando, and Power Morphicon 2012 in California.

In 2016, along with Mason and Cris Andrade, he became the co-owner of Affirmative Clothing that designs mostly inspired by Power Rangers. The following year, he starred in the short film, The Order alongside other former Power Rangers co-stars.
